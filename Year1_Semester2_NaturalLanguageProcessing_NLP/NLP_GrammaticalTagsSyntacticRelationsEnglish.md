# 🔖 Cheat Sheet – Grammatical Tags and Syntactic Relations

# 📖 Parts of Speech + Roles in Dependency Analysis

**Simple rules:**
* **The part of speech tells what the word is**;
* **The syntactic relation tells what it does in the sentence**.

## 📑 1 Parts of Speech (what kind of word it is)

| Tag   | Meaning                     | Example                      |
|-------|----------------------------|------------------------------|
| NOUN  | noun                       | house, fog, lighthouse       |
| PRON  | pronoun                    | he, she, it                  |
| VERB  | verb                       | go, sing, glow               |
| AUX   | auxiliary verb             | is, was, have                |
| ADJ   | adjective                  | cold, old, beautiful         |
| ADV   | adverb                     | quickly, very, silently      |
| DET   | determiner / article       | a, an, the, this             |
| ADP   | preposition                | in, on, through, with        |
| PART  | particle                   | to, not                      |
| SCONJ | subordinating conjunction  | when, because, if            |
| CCONJ | coordinating conjunction   | and, but, or                 |

---

## 📑 2 Syntactic Relations (what role it has in the sentence)

| Tag      | Meaning                   | Role                          | Short Example              |
|----------|---------------------------|-------------------------------|----------------------------|
| nsubj    | nominal subject           | subject                       | It was cold                |
| obj      | object                    | direct object                 | read a book                |
| iobj     | indirect object           | indirect object               | gave him a book            |
| pobj     | object of preposition     | object of preposition         | through the fog            |
| prep     | prepositional relation    | link via preposition          | glow through the fog       |
| det      | determiner                | determines the noun           | the house                  |
| amod     | adjectival modifier       | adjective describing          | cold evening               |
| advmod   | adverbial modifier        | adverbial modifier            | runs quickly               |
| attr     | attribute                 | predicate noun                | It was an evening          |
| aux      | auxiliary                 | verbal auxiliary              | has gone                   |
| xcomp    | open clausal complement   | verb completing another verb  | began to glow              |
| ccomp    | clausal complement        | complement clause             | I know that he left        |
| advcl    | adverbial clause          | adverbial subordinate         | smiled when he arrived     |
| conj     | conjunct                  | coordinated term              | cats and dogs              |
| cc       | coordinating conjunction  | coordinating conjunction      | and / but / or             |
| compound | compound                  | part of compound term         | coffee cup                 |
| case     | case marker / preposition | introduces noun phrase        | in the house               |
